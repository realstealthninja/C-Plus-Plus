\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
An algorithm to divide two numbers under modulo p \href{https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/modular-division}{\texttt{ Modular Division}} 

To calculate division of two numbers under modulo p Modulo operator is not distributive under division, therefore we first have to calculate the inverse of divisor using \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermat\%27s_little_theorem}{\texttt{ Fermat\textquotesingle{}s little theorem}} Now, we can multiply the dividend with the inverse of divisor and modulo is distributive over multiplication operation. Let, We have 3 numbers a, b, p To compute (a/b)p (a/b)p ≡ (a\texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*}(inverse(b)))p ≡ ((ap)\texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*}inverse(b)p)p NOTE\+: For the existence of inverse of \textquotesingle{}b\textquotesingle{}, \textquotesingle{}b\textquotesingle{} and \textquotesingle{}p\textquotesingle{} must be coprime For simplicity we take p as prime Time Complexity\+: O(log(b)) Example\+: ( 24 / 3 ) \% 5 =\texorpdfstring{$>$}{>} 8 \% 5 = 3 --- (i) Now the inverse of 3 is 2 (24 \texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*} 2) \% 5 = (24 \% 5) \texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*} (2 \% 5) = (4 \texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*} 2) \% 5 = 3 --- (ii) (i) and (ii) are equal hence the answer is correct. \begin{DoxySeeAlso}{See also}
