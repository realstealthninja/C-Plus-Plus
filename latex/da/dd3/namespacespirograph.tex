void spirograph\+::spirograph (\begin{DoxyParamCaption}\item[{std\+::array$<$ std\+::pair$<$ double, double $>$, N $>$ \texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*}}]{points}{, }\item[{double}]{l}{, }\item[{double}]{k}{, }\item[{double}]{rot}{}\end{DoxyParamCaption})}

Generate spirograph curve into arrays {\ttfamily x} and {\ttfamily y} such that the i\texorpdfstring{$^\wedge$}{\string^}th point in 2D is represented by {\ttfamily (x\mbox{[}i\mbox{]},y\mbox{[}i\mbox{]})}. The generating function is given by\+:     \begin{eqnarray*}
x &=& R\left[ (1-k) \cos (t) + l\cdot k\cdot\cos \left(\frac{1-k}{k}t\right)
\right]\\
y &=& R\left[ (1-k) \sin (t) - l\cdot k\cdot\sin \left(\frac{1-k}{k}t\right)
\right] \end{eqnarray*} where
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item $R$ is the scaling parameter that we will consider $=1$
\item $l=\frac{\rho}{r}$ is the relative distance of marker from the centre of inner circle and $0\le l\le1$
\item $\rho$ is physical distance of marker from centre of inner circle
\item $r$ is the radius of inner circle
\item $k=\frac{r}{R}$ is the ratio of radius of inner circle to outer circle and $0<k<1$
\item $R$ is the radius of outer circle
\item $t$ is the angle of rotation of the point i.\+e., represents the time parameter
\end{DoxyItemize}

Since we are considering ratios, the actual values of $r$ and $R$ are immaterial.
